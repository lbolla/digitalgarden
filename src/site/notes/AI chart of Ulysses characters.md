The _Circe_ chapter of [[Books/Ulysses\|Ulysses]] features lots of characters from earlier in the book. I needed some help remembering them all, so I asked Gemini to draw a chart of all characters in [[Books/Ulysses\|Ulysses]] and their relationships.

Here is my first prompt:

> Draw a diagram of all characters in Ulysses. Draw lines connecting them if they have a direct relationship in the story

It produced this chart.
![Gemini_Generated_Image_Ulysses_01.png\|640](/img/user/Attachments/Gemini_Generated_Image_Ulysses_01.png)
First of all, I love the style: the yellowish background reminds me of an old drawing; the characters dresses and hairstyle is very much early 1900.
But, the chart itself has some issues: for example, characters are duplicated (e.g. Dennis Breen) and some relations seem wrong. I tried another prompt.

> Nice chart. But some names appear twice like Ellen Bloom or Dennis Breen. Make sure to get the relationships right. I want you to add all Homeric parallel characters like circe Polyphemus, Hades etc

![Gemini_Generated_Image_Ulysses_02.png\|640](/img/user/Attachments/Gemini_Generated_Image_Ulysses_02.png)

Better, but I don't see _Polyphemus_ (The Citizen) or _Circe_ (Bella Cohen). I don't know what _Athena_ is doing there. Dennis Breen is still duplicated. I tried again.

> Bella Cohen should be linked to Circe. Please review the associations

![Gemini_Generated_Image_Ulysses_03.png\|640](/img/user/Attachments/Gemini_Generated_Image_Ulysses_03.png)

Pretty much the same. I was hoping for something better from Gemini: after all, [[Books/Ulysses\|Ulysses]] is now in the open domain, and certainly part of Gemini's training data. I think curated diagrams, like the ones available at [LitCharts](https://www.litcharts.com/lit/ulysses) are better. Will they always be better, or will Gemini (or other AIs) surpass the quality of curated content?
