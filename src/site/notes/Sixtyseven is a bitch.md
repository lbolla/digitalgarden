In _Circe_ [[Books/Ulysses\|Ulysses]], one reads:
> He's getting his pleasure. You won't get a virgin in the flash houses. Ten shillings. Don't be all night before the polis in plain clothes sees us. Sixtyseven is a bitch.

"Sixtyseven is a bitch". What could it mean? I found a brilliant analysis of [what it could possibly mean](https://www.jstor.org/stable/25486521?seq=1). Some explanations are pretty obscure, others boringly trivial.

I asked a boy, he replied: ["Six seveeeeen!"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/6-7_meme).
